Honey Bees live in the hives and create the honey that we consume. They are smaller than bumble bees, and sometimes may be confused for wasps or hornets. Like some human social systems, honey bees work in a caste system.
  1. Queen Bee (female): Typically stays in one spot, gives birth to the larvae to maintain the population of the hive
  2. Worker Bee (female): tend to the queens needs (food, water, etc. as well as protecting the larvae)
  3. Drone Bee (male): go out and find flowers to pollinate
Honey Bees typically do not sting unless they feel that their hive is threatened. They will pursue you in long distances if need be, so be careful!
